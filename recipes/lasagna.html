Lasagna

A Classic Italian Baked Dish

Lasagna is a classic Italian baked dish made of stacked layers of wide, flat pasta sheets, alternating with fillings such as ragù (meat sauce), béchamel sauce, vegetables, and various cheeses like ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan. The assembled dish is typically topped with grated cheese and baked until golden and bubbling.

The dish originated in Italy, with early recipes dating back to the 14th century, though they did not resemble the tomato-based version widely known today, as tomatoes were a later addition to Italian cuisine in the 16th century. True Italian lasagne is renowned for its regional variations; for instance, the classic Bolognese version features a rich meat sauce and béchamel, while a Genoan variation incorporates pesto. Generally, the dish requires careful layering, often starting with sauce on the bottom of the pan to prevent sticking, followed by pasta sheets and fillings, repeated for several layers.
