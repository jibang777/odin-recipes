Grilled Pizza

Pizza is a popular Italian dish featuring a baked, flattened bread dough base topped with tomato sauce, cheese (like mozzarella), and various ingredients such as meats, vegetables, herbs, and spices, originating from Naples and now enjoyed globally in many forms. It's known for its savory flavor, comforting texture, and adaptability to diverse tastes, evolving from ancient flatbreads into a worldwide favorite. .

Grilled pizza involves cooking dough directly on hot grill grates, creating a smoky, charred crust similar to Neapolitan style, and is made by par-cooking the dough, flipping it, quickly adding toppings like sauce and cheese to the cooked side, then finishing on indirect heat until melted and bubbly. The key technique is to cook the dough on one side first, then flip, top quickly, and move to a cooler zone to melt toppings without burning the bottom.

Ingredients

  • Pizza Dough
  • Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • Sauce
  • Cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat Grill: Get your grill hot, ideally with two head zones (direct and indirect).
  2. Oil & Grill Dough: Brush dough with olive oil and place directly on hot grates; grill for a couple of minutes until golden and marked.
  3. Flip & Top: Flip the dough, then quickly add toppings (sauce, cheese, etc.) to the grilled side.
  4. Finish Cooking: Move to indirect heat (or cover the grill) and cook until the cheese melts and the bottom is crisp.
